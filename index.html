About Me

I am an Associate Professor at China Agricultural University, working at the intersection of GIS, environmental science, agricultural economics, and sustainability analytics. My research centers on two major directions:

  • Spatiotemporal process modeling of agriculturalâ€“environmental systems
  • Optimization and governance of sustainable agri-food systems

These fundings collectively support my long-term research agenda on spatiotemporal process modeling and the sustainable transformation of agri-food systems.

Research Directions

1. Spatiotemporal Process Modeling of Agriculturalâ€“Environmental Systems

I develop spatially explicit models to understand how agricultural production, pollutant migration, ecological processes, and human behaviors evolve across space and time. By integrating GIS, remote sensing, spatial statistics, and system simulation, my work uncovers heterogeneity, dynamics, and feedbacks within coupled humanâ€“environment systems.

2. Optimization & Governance of Sustainable Agri-Food Systems

I design optimization and policy frameworks to achieve low-carbon development, heavy-metal pollution governance, and biodiversity protection. My research quantifies the environmental footprints embedded in interprovincial food circulation and proposes implementable solutions that support Chinaâ€™s dual-carbon goals, food safety strategies, and ecological compensation mechanisms.

Methods & Tools

  • Spatial modeling: GIS, Remote Sensing, GeoDetector, spatial regression
  • Optimization: Linear programming, multi-objective optimization, heuristic algorithms (GA, ACO)
  • Machine learning: Random Forest, LSTM, Seq2Seq
  • Environmental & exposure models: InVEST, DNDC, exposure assessment frameworks
